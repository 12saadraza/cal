Emission Report

Scope 1: Direct Emissions

Scope 1 covers direct greenhouse gas emissions from owned or controlled sources. This includes emissions from:

    Fuel combustion in company vehicles
    On-site energy generation
    Process emissions from manufacturing

Example: A manufacturing plant emitting CO2 from its boilers.

Scope 2: Indirect Emissions from Energy

Scope 2 includes indirect emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, steam, heating, and cooling consumed by the reporting company. This accounts for:

  • Electricity consumed in facilities
  • Heating and cooling used in operations

Example: Emissions from the power plants that generate electricity consumed by the company.

  • Emissions from the production of purchased goods and services
  • Transportation and distribution of products
  • Waste disposal and treatment
  • Employee commuting

Example: Emissions from the supply chain, including raw material extraction and transportation.

Summary

This report outlines the three scopes of emissions that organizations must consider in their sustainability efforts. Understanding and managing these emissions is crucial for reducing overall carbon footprints and achieving sustainability goals.
