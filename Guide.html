Level of Emissions

Learn how to use the GHG calculator and understand emissions from different scopes.

Steps to Use the GHG Calculator

  1. Input Data: Enter the necessary data in the calculator, such as fuel consumption, electricity usage, and staff or student commute information.
  2. Select Scope: Choose the relevant scope of emissions (Scope 1, Scope 2, or Scope 3) based on the activity you're tracking.
  3. Review Results: After entering the data, click the "Calculate" button to get the estimated carbon emissions for each scope.
  4. Adjust Parameters: You can adjust the data to see how different factors impact emissions and optimize your strategies for reduction.
  5. Save & Track: Results can be saved for future reference, allowing you to track changes and improvements over time.

Scope 1 Emissions

Scope 1 includes direct emissions from sources that are owned or controlled by an organization, such as emissions from company-owned vehicles, equipment, and facilities.

Scope 2 Emissions

Scope 2 refers to indirect emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, heating, and cooling. These emissions occur at the utility's facilities but are attributed to the organization purchasing the energy.

Scope 3 Emissions

Scope 3 includes all other indirect emissions in the value chain, such as emissions from employee commuting, business travel, purchased goods and services, waste disposal, and product transportation.

Embracing responsibility for emissions at all levels helps build a more sustainable future.
