Understanding Emission Scopes

Exploring the Different Levels of Carbon Emissions for a Sustainable Future

Scope 1 Emissions

Scope 1 includes direct emissions from sources that are owned or controlled by an organization. Examples include emissions from fuel combustion in company-owned vehicles, equipment, and facilities.

Scope 2 Emissions

Scope 2 refers to indirect emissions from the generation of purchased electricity, heating, and cooling. While the emissions occur at the utility's facilities, they are attributed to the purchasing organization.

Scope 3 Emissions

Scope 3 encompasses all other indirect emissions that occur in the value chain, both upstream and downstream. This includes emissions from business travel, purchased goods and services, waste disposal, and the transportation and distribution of products.

Embracing responsibility for emissions at all levels helps build a more sustainable future.
